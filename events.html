CAD Vibe-a-thon Details

Description

A leading automobile OEM is seeking an AI-driven solution to convert large volumes of 2D engineering drawings (PDF format) into accurate 3D models and dimensional visualization outputs. The dataset includes mechanical components, sheet metal parts, and electrical circuit drawings, where manual conversion currently requires significant time and effort.

Hackathon Challenge

Develop an AI-based system capable of dynamically converting 2D PDF engineering drawings into precise and dimensionally accurate 3D models.

Technical Requirements
  • AI-based 2D to 3D conversion approach
  • Compatibility with Siemens NX workflow
  • Multi-format export capability: PRT, GLTF/GLB, OBJ, FBX, STL, PLY
  • Participants are allowed to use any AI tools, frameworks, or models

Pac Bot Rules

  • Teams: 1-3 members
  • Max size: 30 cm × 30 cm × 30 cm
  • Max weight: 5 kg
  • Roles: One bot is Ghost, the other is Pac
  • Objective: Pac bot must reach destination without being caught
  • Arena: Maze environment
  • Ghost wins if it catches Pac; Pac wins if it reaches destination
  • Registration: Online & on-spot available

Robo Rush Rules

  • Max size: 30 cm × 30 cm × 30 cm
  • Max weight: 5 kg
  • Task: Traverse the given path
  • Teams: 1-3 members
  • Event Format: One-time round, no trial allowed
  • Registration: Online & on-spot available
  • Mandatory: Participants must bring college ID card

Robo Soccer Rules

  • Max size: 30 cm × 30 cm × 30 cm
  • Max weight: 5 kg
  • Control: Wired, wireless, or autonomous
  • Power Supply: Self-powered, onboard, max 12V DC
  • Only one participant controls the robot
  • No player exchange during match
  • Teams: Up to 3 members
  • Ball Handling: Push or hit only (no grabbing/holding)
  • Judge's decision is final
  • Online & on-spot registration available

Robo Sumo Rules

  • Max size: 30 cm × 30 cm × 30 cm
  • Max weight: 5 kg
  • Teams: 1-3 members, one robot per team
  • Goal: Push opponent out of ring or immobilize them
  • Match duration: 3 minutes
  • Robots must stay in arena
  • Control: Remote-controlled (wired or wireless)
  • Win by staying in ring or pushing opponent out
  • Safety: No sharp parts or dangerous materials
  • Online & on-spot registration available
